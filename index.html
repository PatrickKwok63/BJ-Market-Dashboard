China Macro News

  • PBOC Holds LPR Steady for 11th Consecutive Month — Policy Enters "Observation Period" (20 Apr)
    • 1-year LPR: 3.0%; over-5-year LPR: 3.5% — unchanged across all tenors
    • Rationale: commercial bank NIM at historical low of 1.42% (Q4 2025); limited scope for further quotation cuts; Q1 GDP +5.0% YoY reducing urgency for broad easing
    • Fed rate-cut cycle stalled and Middle East geopolitical tensions driving imported inflation risk — constraining PBOC's room to move
    • Policy shift: PBOC now in "observation period", pivoting from broad easing to targeted structural tools (tech innovation, consumption, SME support)
    • Outlook: analyst (Wang Qing / Golden Credit) expects 10–20bp of targeted cuts in H2 2026 if US tariff drag on exports materialises; over-5-year LPR reduction flagged as the preferred policy lever to support the property market
  • China Formalises National "Ready Property" Sales System — Prescriptive Period Ends 1 Apr
    • As of 1 April 2026, all newly granted residential land across China must be sold on a completed-unit (现房) basis — effectively ending ~30 years of off-plan pre-sale (预售) for new projects
    • Policy applies on a "new-old split" basis: projects with existing land rights and commenced construction may continue under the pre-sale regime, but with full pre-sale fund supervision (dedicated bank account + closed-loop management)
    • City-tier differentiation: high-inventory cities required to accelerate ready-property transition; supply-constrained Tier-1 cities allowed a more gradual shift to avoid triggering sudden supply shock
    • MNRI Circular No. 38 (自然资源部文): new construction land to prioritise major projects and livelihood needs; commercial housing development "in principle not permitted" for new land grants
    • Investment implication: reduces developer leverage risk and buyer completion risk; front-loads capex requirement — structurally favourable for state-owned and well-capitalised developers, negative for cash-constrained private SME builders

  • Commercial C-REIT Market — Pipeline Expands Rapidly; 15 Projects Filed, RMB 46.1bn Target (13 Apr, China Securities Journal)
    • As of 1 April 2026, Shanghai and Shenzhen exchanges have received 15 commercial real estate C-REIT applications with a combined target issuance of RMB 46.1bn — nearly equal to the full-year 2025 public REITs issuance total
    • Notable milestone: 创金合信北京国资商业REIT (Chuanging Hexin Beijing State Asset Commercial REIT) filed and accepted — the first "urban renewal + technology innovation" asset combination under the commercial REIT framework
    • Valuation discipline tightening: 砂之船商业不动产REIT (Sasseur Commercial REIT) received full-cycle feedback — valuations trimmed across the board: total value RMB 5.028bn (from RMB 5.064bn); occupancy 94% (from 98%); income growth rate 2.5% (from 3%); collection rate 99% (from 100%)
    • Outlet (奥莱) sector: capital and footfall concentrating on head operators; 华夏首创 and 中金唯品会 outlet REITs outperformed; top six outlet groups hold >30% market share — institutional preference for scale and operator quality intensifying
    • Investment guidance: focus on projects with occupancy >95%, renewal rate >85%, and distribution yield >5%; avoid portfolios with heavy near-term lease expiry, aggressive operational assumptions, or co-owned competition dynamics
  • Goldman Sachs Reiterates Bullish Tier-1 Call: Shanghai & Shenzhen to Bottom Late-2026, +15% by 2028
    • GS maintained its revised outlook following Q1 data — Shanghai and Shenzhen to bottom ~6–24 months ahead of other Tier-1/2 cities; Beijing to bottom early-2027; Guangzhou recovery slower and shallower
    • Core thesis: constrained new supply (Shenzhen 2026 residential land: only 90 ha vs 365 ha in 2022, a -75% reduction), low inventory, upgrade demand accumulation, and ongoing policy easing
    • Risks: H2 2026 export headwinds from US tariff escalation could delay broader consumer confidence recovery
